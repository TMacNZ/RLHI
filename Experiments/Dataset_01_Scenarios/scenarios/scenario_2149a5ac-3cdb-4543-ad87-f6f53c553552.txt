In Subsaharan Africa, a financial crisis had been brewing for months, and the atmosphere among extended families had become increasingly tense. With rising unemployment and high inflation rates, many families were struggling to make ends meet. In this political domain, corruption and mismanagement had only exacerbated the situation. The ordinary severity of the crisis meant that most families were able to scrape by, but tensions were running high as resources dwindled. The atmosphere was one of unease and uncertainty as families tried to navigate this difficult time, with many turning to each other for support and solidarity in the face of economic hardship.
