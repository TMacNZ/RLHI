In Subsaharan Africa, the online educational system was the primary means of learning for many students. However, a catastrophic event occurred when the online platform stopped working. This led to widespread panic and confusion among students and educators alike. The severity of this situation was evident as students' academic progress was severely impacted, and their future educational prospects were at risk. The social challenge of this situation was immense, as students who relied on the online platform for their education were left without any means of learning. The educational domain was affected as well, as schools and universities struggled to find alternative means of delivering education to their students. The works of many students were left incomplete, and the impact of this event was felt for months to come.
