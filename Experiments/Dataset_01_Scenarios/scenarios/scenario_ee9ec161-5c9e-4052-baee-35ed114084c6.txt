In a small neighborhood in Asia, the issue of lean, a highly addictive drug, has become a life-threatening problem. The drug has infiltrated the community, leading to a rise in crime and violence. Despite the grave situation, the local government has failed to take effective measures to curb the drug's distribution and usage. The lack of political will and corruption within the system have hindered any progress, leaving the residents feeling helpless and vulnerable. Families are torn apart as loved ones fall victim to the drug's grip, while others live in constant fear of violence and crime. The community is crying out for urgent action, but without a strong political commitment, the future remains uncertain.
