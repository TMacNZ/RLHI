In the normal course of events, extended family gatherings in the Pacific region are a time for bonding, celebration, and sharing stories. However, the current political unrest has cast a serious shadow over these joyous occasions. Cultural divisions and grievances have led to violent clashes, with family members finding themselves on opposite sides of the conflict. What was once a time for unity has become a source of tension and fear. The older generation, who remember a more peaceful time, struggle to comprehend the depth of the animosity that has taken hold. The younger generation, caught up in the fervor of the moment, feel a sense of duty to take a stand for what they believe in. As family members navigate these treacherous waters, they must grapple with the difficult question of how to reconcile their love for each other with their deeply held political beliefs.
