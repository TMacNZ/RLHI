In Asia, there has been an increasing trend of incorporating traditional beliefs and practices into educational curriculum. One such example is the inclusion of the concept of God in the study of environmental issues. While the severity of this inclusion may be insignificant, it has nevertheless sparked debate among educators and policymakers. Some argue that the concept of God has no place in a scientific discussion of environmental concerns, while others believe that it can serve as a valuable cultural lens through which to view the topic. Regardless of the debate, this trend highlights the importance of understanding the complex intersection of culture and education in the region.
