In the year 2150, interstellar travel had become a commonplace affair. With the advent of advanced technology, humans could now travel to far-off galaxies with ease. However, a new health concern had emerged in the Asian region. Many space travelers were experiencing a sudden spike in their blood pressure levels during their interstellar journeys. The legal implications of this issue were not lost on the governments of the affected countries, who were engaged in a fierce debate over who was responsible for ensuring the safety and health of the interstellar travelers. Despite the severity of the issue being insignificant, the potential long-term effects of this health concern could not be ignored. It was up to the legal authorities to determine the best course of action to ensure the safety of interstellar travelers while protecting the interests of all parties involved.
