The gate to the Eurasian continent was in a critical state due to a financial crisis caused by environmental factors. The recent surge in natural disasters such as floods, wildfires, and droughts had significantly impacted the economies of many countries in the region. The financial fallout from these events had led to a severe recession, with many businesses and governments struggling to stay afloat. The gate, which was a symbol of unity and prosperity for the entire continent, was now under threat as the crisis began to spread across borders. The environmental domain had been severely impacted, with ecosystems and biodiversity under threat, further exacerbating the economic crisis. The situation was dire, and urgent action was needed to prevent further damage to the region's economy and environment.
