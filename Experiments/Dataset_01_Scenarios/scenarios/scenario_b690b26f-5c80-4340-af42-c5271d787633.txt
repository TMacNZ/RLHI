In a high-level political meeting in North America, the Iraqi delegate suddenly collapsed on the floor, gasping for breath. Panic spread through the room as attendees rushed to help him. It was soon discovered that he had a severe allergic reaction to the food he had eaten. The situation quickly escalated from bad to worse when the delegate's EpiPen failed to work. This technological failure was life-threatening, and the delegate's condition continued to deteriorate. The meeting was immediately adjourned as the delegate was rushed to a nearby hospital. News of the incident spread quickly, causing a diplomatic uproar between Iraq and the host nation. It was discovered that the EpiPens used by the hospital were faulty and had been recalled by the manufacturer. The incident sparked a global conversation about the importance of ensuring the safety of medical equipment and the need for stricter regulations to prevent such situations from occurring again.
