In the aftermath of a devastating earthquake that hit the Pacific region, the federal government announced emergency protocols to manage the dangerous situation. The earthquake had triggered a massive tsunami that had left several coastal towns and cities in ruins. The live footage of the destruction that was broadcasted on national TV channels had left the entire country in shock. The President had declared a state of emergency and authorized the deployment of the National Guard to assist in search and rescue missions. The political implications of this disaster were of grave concern, as several politicians called for an investigation into the government's preparedness and response to the earthquake. Meanwhile, the residents of the affected areas were left to pick up the pieces of their shattered lives, with many displaced and homeless.
