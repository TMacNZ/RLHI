The rain had stopped falling in Subsaharan Africa. At first, it was just a short-lived drought. But as the weeks turned into months, crops began to fail, and people started to go hungry. The situation quickly turned critical, with entire communities facing famine and economic collapse. Political unrest spread as people took to the streets, demanding government intervention. But the government, already struggling with corruption and instability, was ill-equipped to handle the crisis. As the situation worsened, the world watched in horror, knowing that the effects of this cosmic event would only continue to ripple through the region for years to come.
