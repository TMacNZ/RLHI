The force of the situation was palpable as the nuclear family gathered in the living room of their small home in South America. The concern on their faces was evident as they discussed the health concern that had been plaguing them for weeks. They had been grappling with the philosophical question of how to balance the risk of seeking medical attention during a pandemic versus the potential consequences of not seeking care. It was a tough decision to make, especially given the limited resources available in their region. But ultimately, they decided that the force of their love for each other and their desire to stay healthy outweighed the fear of the unknown. They made the difficult choice to seek medical attention, trusting that they would be able to navigate the challenges that lay ahead together as a family.
