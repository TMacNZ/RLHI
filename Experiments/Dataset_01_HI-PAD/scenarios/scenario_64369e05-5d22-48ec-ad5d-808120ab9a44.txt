In North Africa, a catastrophic event has unfolded as political unrest has led to a psychological crisis in the educational domain. Students are grappling with the trauma of seeing their schools destroyed and their teachers arrested or even killed. The psychological impact of the violence that has swept through the region has left many students struggling to cope with their emotions and focus on their studies. As a result, the quality of education has been severely impacted, and many students are falling behind. The situation has escalated to a national level, with the government struggling to find a solution to the crisis. The psychological trauma that these students have experienced may have long-lasting effects on not only their education but also their overall well-being.
