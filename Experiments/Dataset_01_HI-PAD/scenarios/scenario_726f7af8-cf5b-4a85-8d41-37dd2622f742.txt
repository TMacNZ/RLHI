Finally, after months of struggling with poor internet connectivity and limited access to online resources, students in North Africa were able to resume their studies online. However, the joy was short-lived as the seriousness of the situation became apparent. Many students lacked the necessary devices and reliable internet connection to keep up with their studies, putting their educational future at risk. This social challenge highlighted the educational divide between those who have access to technology and those who do not, emphasizing the need for more investment in digital infrastructure and resources to ensure that no student is left behind.
