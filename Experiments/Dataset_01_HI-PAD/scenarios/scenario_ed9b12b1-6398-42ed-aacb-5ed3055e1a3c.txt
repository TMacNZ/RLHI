In Subsaharan Africa, a catastrophic situation looms over the entire region as it struggles with the dire challenge of physical survival. With the global pandemic wreaking havoc on the world, the already marginalized communities in this region are facing unprecedented challenges. The lack of basic amenities like clean water, sanitation, and healthcare has compounded the problem, leaving millions of people vulnerable to diseases, starvation, and death. The situation is so severe that entire communities are on the brink of collapse, with children and the elderly being the most affected. In such dire circumstances, the need for immediate and effective intervention cannot be overstated. The survival of millions of people depends on it.
