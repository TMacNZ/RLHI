In many parts of Asia, small chosen families face a typical social challenge related to economic domain. Due to limited financial resources, small families often struggle to make ends meet and provide for their basic needs. This can lead to feelings of stress and anxiety, as well as difficulty accessing healthcare and education. In some cases, small chosen families may need to rely on informal support networks, such as friends and neighbors, to help them navigate these challenges. Despite these obstacles, many small chosen families in Asia remain resilient and committed to supporting one another through difficult times. By working together and leveraging their collective strengths, they are able to overcome economic hardships and build a brighter future for themselves and their loved ones.
