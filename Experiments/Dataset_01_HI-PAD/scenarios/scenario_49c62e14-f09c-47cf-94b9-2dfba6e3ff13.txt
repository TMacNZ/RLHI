As the Olympic games draw near, a critical health concern has arisen in Europe. Philosophical debates have sparked among officials regarding the responsibility of the continental organization to ensure the safety of athletes and spectators amidst the ongoing pandemic. With the global health crisis still looming, the decision to proceed with the international event has been met with controversy. While some argue that the spirit of the Olympic games encourages unity and perseverance in the face of adversity, others fear that the gathering of individuals from all corners of the world could lead to a surge in COVID-19 cases. As the clock ticks down to the opening ceremony, the question remains: is the risk worth the reward?
