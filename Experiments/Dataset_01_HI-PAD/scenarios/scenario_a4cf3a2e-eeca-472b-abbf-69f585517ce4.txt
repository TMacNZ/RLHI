The emotion of fear had been long-lasting in the Middle East due to the ongoing conflict and violence that plagued the region. Students attending schools in the region were not immune to the effects of this fear, as it seeped into their daily lives and learning experiences. Teachers struggled to provide a safe and stable environment for their students, often having to cancel classes due to unexpected outbreaks of violence. Students were apprehensive about attending school, knowing that they could be putting themselves in danger. The emotional toll of these circumstances weighed heavily on the students, affecting their mental health and ability to learn. Despite these challenges, educators in the region remained committed to providing their students with a quality education, working tirelessly to mitigate the impact of the ongoing conflict and violence on their students' emotional and academic well-being.
